# GENEVIEVE App™ Dog Park
## Voluntary Check-In and Check-Out Council Pilot Proposal

**Prepared for discussion with local government — 14 July 2026**

## Proposal

Run a limited voluntary digital pilot at selected dog exercise areas. Owners may choose to record:

- dog arrival and expected visit length;
- current play/safety status;
- needs-space, on-lead or in-training indicators;
- supervision confirmations; and
- voluntary departure.

Participation is not a condition of entry, and no penalty, access restriction or negative profile consequence applies when an owner chooses not to participate.

## Why begin voluntarily

A voluntary pilot allows council and the community to test usefulness, privacy, accessibility, uptake and data quality before considering any formal policy or regulatory change.

The app must clearly distinguish:

- official council rules and verified notices;
- voluntary community reports;
- estimated crowd information; and
- emergency or incident records.

## Safeguards

1. **No exact public owner location by default.**
2. **Automatic expiry** of check-ins after the owner-selected visit length.
3. **Manual check-out remains optional.**
4. **Counts are labelled estimates**, not official headcounts or guaranteed capacity.
5. **No transfer of care or supervision** to the app or council.
6. **No mandatory participation without formal authority, consultation and legal/privacy review.**
7. **Role separation** between public users, owners, park superintendent/council operators and authorised responders.
8. **Restricted dog medical and owner contact information is not shown in public or ordinary operator views.**

## Possible 90-day pilot

- Select 1–3 parks with different sizes and usage patterns.
- Add optional QR signage explaining the pilot.
- Publish official park rules and facility details through the council/operator channel.
- Invite users to opt in.
- Review results at 30, 60 and 90 days.

## Measures

- percentage of visitors choosing to participate;
- completion versus automatic expiry;
- average visit length;
- reported gate crowding;
- heat and hazard alerts viewed;
- needs-space and in-training indicators used;
- supervision reminders acknowledged;
- incident and near-miss reports;
- privacy or accessibility complaints;
- owner, ranger and maintenance-team feedback; and
- whether the information improved decisions without creating false certainty.

## Council participation requested

- verify park names, boundaries, opening hours and rules;
- nominate a responsible operator contact;
- review privacy and records handling;
- approve pilot signage and public wording;
- identify maintenance and incident escalation pathways;
- decide whether aggregated data can assist planning;
- determine whether any future mandatory model is legally justified and proportionate.

## Future mandatory use

The app is currently configured as **voluntary**. It must not be described as legally required unless a competent authority formally adopts the requirement and the authority, legal instrument, effective date, privacy arrangements, accessibility alternatives and enforcement position are verified and published.
